SPIRE

The City Must Fall

You are drow. Second-class citizens in a city your ancestors built. Secret rebels in a war the city doesn't know is happening yet.

Enter the Spire

The City

High above the ground, in a city of iron and shadow and spite, the Aelfir rule. They came three hundred years ago. They never left.

The Spire is a mile-high tower-city — a monument to power, excess, and the slow violence of occupation. At the highest levels, the Aelfir, the high elves, live in gilded brutality. Below them, deeper in the dark, the drow endure.

You are one of the drow. Member of a secret Ministry with its own agenda, methods, and price. You will work toward the fall of your occupiers through espionage, violence, political subversion, or stranger means. You will pay for it. The question is how much you're willing to lose.

"You're not heroes. You're desperate people in an impossible situation, making terrible choices for reasons that feel good enough."

The World

A city of impossible height. A war fought in whispers and shadows.

Occupiers

The Aelfir

The high elves who conquered the Spire three centuries ago and built an entire culture atop drow labour. They rule from the upper levels, conducting their art, politics, and casual cruelty in equal measure.

The Resistance

The Drow

The original builders and inhabitants of the Spire. Now second-class citizens, confined to the lower levels and the forgotten quarters. Most endure. Some remember. A few fight back.

Beneath the City

The Vermissian

A broken rail network beneath the Spire that defies physics and geography alike. Tunnels lead to places that shouldn't exist. The perfect place to run, to hide, or to quietly lose your mind.

The Unseen

The Occult

Magic is real, unstable, and hungry. The Spire is riddled with dark gods, forgotten rituals, and things that should not exist that do anyway — because the city demands them.

The Ministries

Every character belongs to a secret organisation with its own agenda, methods, and price. Choose your allegiance. Live with the consequences.

✦

Our Lady of the Darkness

Religious assassins who serve a forgotten drow goddess. They believe the city must bleed before it can be healed. Faith is their sharpest weapon.

⊕

The Vermissian Scholia

Reality hackers and scholars who exploit the broken geometry of the underground rail. They go places that should not exist — and come back changed.

⚙

The Stranglers

A violent labour union with legitimate grievances and extremely illegitimate methods. Class consciousness, enforced at knifepoint. Solidarity or nothing.

◈

The Forgotten Children

Community organisers, spies, and social workers who protect drow life from the inside. They believe the resistance must be worth living in, not just dying for.

⚖

The Silver Conventicle

Political moderates and lawyers working within the system — for now. They play the long game, leveraging Aelfir law against the Aelfir themselves.

☽

The Bound

Warriors who have made deals with dark powers, binding terrible forces to their will at great personal cost. Every use leaves a mark.

"The Spire has stood for centuries. It will stand for centuries more, unless someone decides it shouldn't — and is willing to pay what that costs." — Spire: The City Must Fall
